# Floppy Duck Support Playbook

## Objective

Turn bugs, confusion, and reviews into faster fixes, better retention, and better App Store sentiment.

## Support Inputs

- App Store reviews
- TestFlight feedback
- email or form submissions
- social comments with bug reports or confusion

## Triage Rules

- `P0`: crash, lost purchases, account deletion failure, sign-in failure blocking play
- `P1`: multiplayer broken, IAP broken, save/progression loss, hard UI blockers
- `P2`: confusing UX, cosmetic bugs, stats mismatches, partial feature failure
- `P3`: polish issues, copy improvements, wishlist requests

## Response Principles

- respond quickly
- confirm what happened in plain language
- do not blame Apple, TestFlight, or the user
- capture repro details before closing the loop
- turn repeated complaints into product or docs changes

## FAQ Backlog

- How do I restore purchases?
- Why do some modes require Sign in with Apple?
- How do I unlock bot reward skins?
- Why can’t I find a ranked match?
- How do I delete my account?
- Why didn’t my score or ELO update?

## Review Response Guidance

- thank the player
- address the concrete issue
- mention when a fix is live or planned
- keep tone human, short, and accountable

## If You Are an AI Agent

### Weekly

- summarize top support themes and repeat questions
- rewrite or expand the FAQ where confusion is rising
- identify one issue hurting ratings that deserves a product fix

### Before launch

- prepare support macros for sign-in, purchases, multiplayer, and delete-account questions
- make sure support copy matches the app’s current flows

### After launch

- publish a weekly support report with issue counts, severity, and recommended actions

## KPIs

- time to first response
- repeat issue count
- rating trend
- percentage of issues closed with a confirmed fix or explanation
