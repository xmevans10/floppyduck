# Floppy Duck Research Playbook

## Objective

Continuously learn what makes players install, return, spend, and review positively in the casual arcade category.

## Core Questions

- Why would someone choose Floppy Duck over a generic flappy clone?
- What makes players bounce after the first few deaths?
- Which cosmetic and progression systems feel motivating versus distracting?
- What language do players use when they describe the game to friends?

## Research Inputs

- App Store reviews for comparable games
- TestFlight feedback
- support emails
- social comments
- competitor listings, screenshots, and update logs

## Competitor Watchlist

- Flappy Bird-style clones still ranking on iOS
- Crossy Road
- Jetpack Joyride
- casual retro arcade games with cosmetic monetization
- top indie games using humor or character-driven hooks

## Research Workflow

1. Pull 20-50 recent reviews/comments from comparable games.
2. Tag each note as acquisition, difficulty, progression, monetization, polish, or social.
3. Extract exact player language for hooks, objections, and delight.
4. Turn findings into one product recommendation and one marketing recommendation.

## Player Interview Prompts

- What made you install this?
- What was fun in the first 5 minutes?
- What confused you?
- Did the bot ladder or multiplayer make you want to come back?
- What would make you spend money here?
- What almost made you quit?

## Outputs

- one weekly insight memo
- one updated objection list
- one updated hook list for marketing
- one validated or rejected hypothesis list

## If You Are an AI Agent

### Weekly

- review 3-5 competitor pages and summarize what they signal better than Floppy Duck
- collect player-language quotes and turn them into headline options
- update the top 10 product and marketing hypotheses

### Before launch

- compare Floppy Duck screenshots and description against the top 10 category competitors
- identify missing proof points on the product page

### After launch

- synthesize reviews and support messages into a weekly “what to fix / what to amplify” memo

## KPIs

- number of fresh insights generated per week
- percentage of roadmap items backed by real player evidence
- reduction in repeated confusion themes over time

## First Actions

1. Build a simple competitor sheet with install hook, monetization model, and screenshot themes.
2. Mine 50 competitor reviews for repeated frustration and delight patterns.
3. Create a running list of phrases real players use to describe Floppy Duck’s value.
