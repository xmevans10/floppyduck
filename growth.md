# Floppy Duck Growth Playbook

## Objective

Run focused growth experiments that increase installs, retention, and cosmetic revenue without sacrificing trust or launch readiness.

## Growth Principles

- fix activation before scaling acquisition
- optimize the product page before spending money on traffic
- prefer experiments that can be reversed quickly
- do not ship manipulative monetization for a casual skill game

## Experiment Areas

- App Store screenshots and subtitle
- first-session progression clarity
- bot ladder surfacing
- daily streak visibility
- shop timing and placement
- share and referral prompts
- social proof from high scores and unlocks

## Prioritization Framework

Rank tests by:

- expected impact on installs, retention, or revenue
- confidence from research
- implementation effort
- compliance or review risk

## Experiment Template

- hypothesis
- change
- primary KPI
- guardrail KPI
- expected timeframe
- stop / continue rule

## Starter Experiment Backlog

- show clearer “beat bots to unlock skins” messaging on first ladder entry
- test a more explicit shop CTA after first meaningful bread milestone
- test App Store screenshots that lead with bot ladder versus multiplayer versus cosmetics
- test a post-best-score share prompt versus no prompt
- test stronger progression copy on the stats screen

## If You Are an AI Agent

### Weekly

- propose 3 experiments with KPI, sample-size assumption, and expected downside
- close out old experiments with a clear decision
- update [marketing.md](/Users/xanderevans/Documents/floppyduck/marketing.md) and [product.md](/Users/xanderevans/Documents/floppyduck/product.md) when learnings affect either side

### Before launch

- focus on listing, activation, and shop visibility
- avoid experiments that introduce new compliance surface or backend risk

### After launch

- keep one acquisition test, one retention test, and one monetization test active at a time

## KPIs

- install rate
- day-1 retention
- shop visit rate
- purchase conversion
- review rating and review volume

## First Actions

1. Rank the first 10 experiments by impact and safety.
2. Start with App Store creative and first-session clarity.
3. Delay paid acquisition until retention and purchase basics look healthy.
