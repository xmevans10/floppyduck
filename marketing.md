# Floppy Duck Marketing Playbook

## Objective

Acquire high-intent players for a free pixel-art iOS game and turn a meaningful share of them into retained players and cosmetic buyers.

## Current Product Facts

- Core hook: Flappy-style one-tap gameplay with a duck theme, pixel-art presentation, and fast restart loop.
- Differentiators already in product: bot ladder, multiplayer, cosmetics, progression, leaderboard, daily streaks.
- Revenue model today: free app with cosmetic IAPs plus bread-based unlocks.
- Privacy posture: no ATT-based targeting should be assumed.

## Positioning

- Primary message: "Flappy Bird energy, but with more reasons to come back."
- Secondary message: "A retro duck game with bot bosses, multiplayer, and collectible cosmetics."
- Tone: playful, competitive, nostalgic, slightly chaotic.

## Outputs

- 3-5 short-form video concepts per week
- 2 App Store creative hypotheses per month
- 1 updated value-prop memo after every major feedback batch
- a rolling backlog of hooks, captions, and thumbnail copy

## KPI Targets

- App Store page view to install rate
- install to first game started rate
- install to day-1 return rate
- install to first purchase rate
- organic share of installs versus paid/tester outreach

## Channel Priorities

### Before launch

- App Store listing quality
- short-form video for TikTok, Reels, Shorts
- founder/devlog posts on X and indie game communities
- direct outreach to friends, creators, and small gaming accounts for TestFlight feedback

### After launch

- repeatable short-form content using score chases, bot reactions, fails, and unlock moments
- App Store screenshot and subtitle iteration
- lightweight community building around best scores, bot clears, and new cosmetic drops

## Creative Angles

- "Can you beat all 8 duck bots?"
- "This looks like Flappy Bird until the ladder, shop, and multiplayer show up."
- "Every asset is generated from code."
- "Beat the boss duck, unlock the skin."
- "I made a duck version of Flappy Bird and added ELO."

## Launch Campaign

- 7 days before TestFlight: start devlog and teaser clips.
- 3 days before TestFlight: show bot ladder and cosmetic unlocks.
- launch day: post one gameplay clip, one progression clip, one multiplayer clip.
- 3 days after launch: publish best tester moments, highest scores, and first feedback changes.
