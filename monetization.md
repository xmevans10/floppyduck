# Floppy Duck Monetization Playbook

## Objective

Grow revenue through cosmetic purchases and better conversion, while preserving the game’s trust, replayability, and App Store safety.

## Current Revenue Surface

- premium skins: Alien, Wizard, Devil
- premium themes: Space, Tokyo
- bread unlocks for some skins, themes, and banners
- bot reward cosmetics that drive retention and status
- code also supports premium banners; launch state must be reconciled with App Store Connect inventory before release

## Pricing Assumptions

- premium skins at impulse-buy pricing
- premium themes and premium banners at slightly higher cosmetic pricing
- bread purchases do not currently exist and should not be assumed
- subscriptions and power sales are out of scope

## What Should Drive Revenue

- identity: cosmetics that make a player feel distinct
- completion: cosmetic sets and collection progress
- status: items linked to ladder, ranked, or rare ownership
- novelty: limited-time creative refreshes after launch

## What Not To Do Before Launch

- do not add ads just to create revenue quickly
- do not add subscriptions without a clear recurring-value loop
- do not gate core play behind payment
- do not ship premium products that are visible in-app but missing in App Store Connect

## Monetization Opportunities

- better shop merchandising for premium versus bread items
- cosmetic bundles after the core standalone items convert
- premium banner strategy if banners are meant to ship in v1
- event-based promos tied to updates, not manipulative countdown spam

## KPIs

- shop view rate
- premium product tap rate
- purchase conversion by product family
- average revenue per paying user
- share of revenue from first-session versus repeat buyers

## If You Are an AI Agent

### Weekly

- report which product family has the best conversion
- identify where players drop between shop view and purchase completion
- recommend one pricing, packaging, or merchandising test

### Before launch

- reconcile visible premium inventory against App Store Connect setup
- verify the review notes and metadata explain where IAPs live in the app

### After launch

- propose one low-risk monetization experiment at a time
- update the pricing and packaging memo as soon as real purchase data exists

## Outputs

- weekly monetization memo
- premium inventory checklist
- merchandising recommendations
- experiment proposals

## First Actions

1. Decide whether premium banners are in or out for the initial TestFlight/App Store wave.
2. Make the App Store Connect product list match that decision exactly.
3. Prioritize conversion improvements over adding new revenue systems.
