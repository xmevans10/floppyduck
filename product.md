# Floppy Duck Product Playbook

## Objective

Improve retention, conversion, and review quality without bloating the core one-tap arcade loop.

## Product Strategy

- Keep the first session simple: tap to flap, score, restart, feel progress fast.
- Use progression to create return reasons: bot ladder, collection, streaks, leaderboards, multiplayer.
- Monetize with cosmetics and status, not pay-to-win.
- Avoid systems that add friction before the first fun moment.

## Player Segments

- Nostalgia players: install for the Flappy-style core loop.
- Competitors: care about leaderboard, ranked, and high scores.
- Collectors: care about skins, themes, banners, and unlock completion.
- Social challengers: want quick multiplayer and private-room bragging rights.

## Priority Rubric

Score each candidate idea on:

- retention impact
- purchase impact
- review-risk reduction
- implementation cost
- launch safety

Prefer work that improves first-session clarity, replayability, or cosmetic conversion without introducing new compliance risk.

## Current Product Loops

- skill loop: play, fail, retry, beat score
- progression loop: earn bread, unlock cosmetics, beat bots
- status loop: rank, leaderboard, rare rewards
- social loop: private rooms, ranked outcomes, share-worthy moments

## Near-Term Product Bets

- make the first 10 minutes more legible and rewarding
- surface the bot ladder and collection more aggressively
- convert delight into shop visits without harming trust
- reinforce streaks and progression in the home/stats flows

## Monetization Principles

- keep premium offers cosmetic
- earn trust before asking for money
- reward play generously enough that bread unlocks feel real
- use premium items for identity and rarity, not power
